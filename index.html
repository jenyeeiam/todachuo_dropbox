Welcome to the one and only English webpage for the Todachuo Softball Team! We represent the Toda Medical Group (TMG), a family of hospitals based out of the Saitama prefecture in Japan. We are one of twelve teams that compete in the Japanese professional league, a part of the Japanese Softball Association (JSA). For those who can read Japanese, or are feeling adventurous, checkout the TMG, Toda and JSA websites!

Toda's Team Code of Conduct

  1. One shall not say/do anything to embarrass the hospital or the softball team. This includes, but is not exhaustive
    • Stealing water from strangers
    • Wearing wet hair in public
    • Shouting in public
    • Laughing in public
    • Having fun in public
  2. One shall wear socks and underarmour at all times
  3. One shall never eat while standing
  4. One shall be awake 5 hours before game time
  5. One shall refrain from eating ramen and raw fish 48 hours before a game. This rule does not apply if eating at Hamanasu
  6. One shall never lose her residence card, as the consequence is JAIL
  7. One shall never forget her game jersey, as the consequence is a 5000 yen fine
  8. One shall never have her phone make noise on the bus, as the consequence is a 200 yen fine
  9. One shall never chew gum at softball
  10. In between games of a double header, each player is given no more than 2 riceballs and 1 pan
  11. One must wear clean clothes and crocs to Hamanasu
  12. One must have a different bag for practice and games
  13. One shall never let her bat handle touch the ground
  14. One shall never throw her bat, glove or any piece of equipment on the ground
  15. If you're not starving, you're not doing it right

Japanese is a hard language to learn. Let's all learn together! Here are some words, phrases and tips vital to survival on a Japanese softball team.

  1. Counting to 10. Probably THE most important thing to know playing softball. The Japanese love counting reps and timing drills!
    • 1 - ichi, 2 - ni, 3 - san, 4 - shi, 5 - go, 6 - roku, 7 - shichi, 8 - hachi, 9 - kyu, 10 - ju, 11 - ju ichi, 12 - ju ni etc etc
  2. Many Japanese words originate from English. Sound more Japanese by saying words twice! For example,
    • yes yes
    • no no
    • many many
    • ok ok
  3. Asking questions - The 5 W's.
    • Who? - Dare?
      • Who is the head coach? = Kantoku wa dare desu ka?
    • What? - Nani/Nan?
      • What is that? Nan desu ka?
    • When? - Itsu?
      • When do you go? Itsu ikimasu ka?
    • Where? - Doko?
      • Where is my riceball? Watashi no onigiri wa doko desu ka?
    • Why? - Naze?
      • Why running? Naze ranningu?
  4. Daijobu desu = I'm ok. Very useful phrase to get out of extra practice, running, or even strange foods!
  5. Complements are easy because any good deed or job well done can be acknowledged with "Nice." For example, "Nice batting," "Nice pitching," "Nice eating," "Nice English."

Now for some vocabulary. Probably the hardest part about communicating in Japan is the inability to come up with the words to express what you want or are looking for. Hover the curser over the picture to learn the words. All Japanese words are written in Romaji, or the "English" form of the word. Here are some rules of thumb for pronounciation.

  • Vowel sounds
    • A = ah, as in hah or law
    • I = ee, as in free or machine
    • U = oo, as in boo or moo
    • E = eh, as in met
    • O = oh, as in so or mow
  • There are no L or V sounds, where L sounds like R, and V sounds like B.
  • R sounds like a mix between R and L, and sort of like D. R in Japanese sounds similar to R in Spanish
  • Double consonants means you pronounce it twice. Ex. Bookkepper.

March 24 2014

After living here for 9+ months, I have gotten to reflect on this country quite a bit. Here are some observations I have made.

  • The ideal height for a girl is 5'4" and your shoe size should not exceed a 9 lest you be limited to men's shoes forever.
  • No high heels are too high.
  • Platform shoes are still in style.
  • Watch your weight, or get a higher metabolism. Clothing does not go above a size 6.
  • Japan is probably the most anti-vegetarian country EVER.
  • Heated toilet seats are common, and welcome by all.
  • No establishment is complete without a sink to wash your hands.
  • Garbage cans are uneccessary eyesores.
  • It's either baseball season or it's not.
  • No outfit is complete without a fanny pack.
  • Service is ALWAYS with a smile.
  • Men should not have facial hair, as you give the impression of being homeless.
  • No workday is too long.
  • No picture is complete without a peace sign.
  • If you look lost for long enough, someone will surely help you.
  • Surgical masks are not just for surgery.
  • Children should not cry. It brings shame to the family.
